Dolphins

What are Dolphins?

A dolphin is a common name used for some of the aquatic mammals
in the cetacean clade Odontoceti, the toothed whales. Dolphins belong
to the families Delphinidae (the oceanic dolphins), along with the river
dolphin families Platanistidae (the Indian river dolphins), Iniidae
(the New World river dolphins), Pontoporiidae (the brackish dolphins),
and probably extinct Lipotidae (baiji or Chinese river dolphin).
There are 40 extant species named as dolphins.
